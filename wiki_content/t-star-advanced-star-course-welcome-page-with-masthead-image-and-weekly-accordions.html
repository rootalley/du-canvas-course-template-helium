What Will I Learn?

Upon the successful completion of this course, you'll be able to:

  • Identify the different roles an accountant can play in that organization.
  • Calculate the variables included in the cost-volume-profit (CVP) model and discuss how they affect the profitability of the organization.
  • Describe the functions of job costing and activity-based costing (ABC) and demonstrate how these techniques are utilized in a managerial capacity.
  • Calculate and use standard costing information to analyze direct material, direct labor and overhead costs.
  • Use the four capital budgeting models to make appropriate investment decisions regarding capital assets.
  • Utilize differential analysis in assisting with managerial decision making with regards to make vs. buy decisions, elimination of a product or division, accepting a special order, etc.
  • Develop a master budget and discuss how the budgets are used in managing the organization.
  • Discuss capacity utilization and issues related to capacity.

Week 1 - A Detailed, Single-Column Format

Objectives

In this module, we will explain the relationship between nutrition and health. Upon completion, you will be able to:

Nutrition in Human Health

  1. Describe the relationship between nutrition and health.
  2. Identify the six classes of nutrients, their functions, and their essentiality.
  3. Recognize the possible relationship of genetics to the adequacy of nutrition.
  4. Compare dietary intakes in the United States with the U.S. Department of Agriculture Dietary Guidelines.
  5. Discuss issues related to food insecurity on local and global levels.
  6. List and describe the steps in providing nutritional care.
  7. Explain the intended use of the Dietary Reference Intakes.
  8. Relate the underlying concept of the Exchange Lists to overall healthful eating.
  9. Give an example of a provider’s use of, and respect for, cultural beliefs having a favorable impact on a health outcome for a client.
  10. State the preferences and dietary restrictions of several cultural and religious groups.

Food Management

  1. Describe the conditions under which microbiologic food illnesses can occur.
  2. Identify foods that are likely to harbor disease-producing microorganisms.
  3. Teach clients how to prevent foodborne illnesses.
  4. Discuss the information on food labels.
